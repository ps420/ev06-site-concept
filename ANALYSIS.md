# Tracklab v2 Audit + EV06 Rebuild Direction

## What Tracklab v2 gets right
- Covers multiple buyer segments: elderly, family, workplace.
- Mentions practical safety features early.
- Shows the product visually.
- Explains some core benefits like GPS, voice calls, battery alerts, and fall detection.
- Establishes that the platform can support both personal and business use cases.

## What weakens the current site

### 1) It feels like a template, not a product launch
- Generic headings like "We've got you covered!"
- Placeholder-style news section with irrelevant dummy content.
- Weak service/business copy under business solutions.
- Repetitive blocks without a clear conversion path.

### 2) The messaging is broad but not sharp
- The hero does not clearly answer:
  - what the device is
  - who it is for
  - why this model is better
  - what action the visitor should take next
- It mixes family, workplace, elderly, business, and platform messaging without a strong hierarchy.

### 3) The product feels undersold
- The wearable is safety-critical, but the presentation does not feel high trust or premium.
- No strong "why choose this over a normal panic button/watch" positioning.
- Not enough emphasis on immediate outcomes: faster response, accurate location, voice verification, peace of mind.

### 4) Visual system feels dated
- Traditional corporate-theme structure.
- Heavy template sections, generic cards, filler spacing.
- Lacks the high-end product-launch feel expected for a modern 4G wearable.

### 5) Weak conversion architecture
- No crisp CTA ladder.
- No obvious lead path like demo / quote / contact / reseller enquiry.
- No structured trust section, no deployment flow, no partner story, no strong FAQ ordering.

## EV06 opportunity
The EV06 should be positioned more like a modern connected safety wearable, not just another panic button.

## Better strategy for the EV06 site

### Positioning
Present EV06 as:
- a 4G wearable safety watch
- built for elderly care, lone-worker protection, and high-risk mobile users
- combining emergency response, live location, and health-aware monitoring

### Core differentiators to emphasize
Based on source material, highlight:
- SOS panic activation
- two-way calling
- heart-rate monitoring
- blood oxygen / wellness telemetry
- step counting
- battery and status visibility
- GPS/location services
- cellular connectivity (2G/3G/4G where available)
- platform compatibility / monitoring-center readiness
- wearable form factor for everyday use

### Better site architecture
1. Hero with immediate value proposition
2. Social proof / trusted use cases
3. Use-case split: seniors / lone workers / family / monitored sites
4. Product feature grid
5. Emergency response workflow
6. Monitoring / platform integration story
7. Technical specs snapshot
8. FAQ
9. Strong CTA band + enquiry form

## Design direction for the rebuild
- Keep dark / premium / technical feel.
- Use cleaner product-launch composition.
- Blend safety-tech seriousness with a modern wearable aesthetic.
- Make the device look premium, trusted, and capable.
- Remove all filler/template junk.
